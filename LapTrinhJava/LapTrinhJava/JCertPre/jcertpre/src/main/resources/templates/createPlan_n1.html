Plan for N1 level

Focus on this path and you will succeed

  • Month 1

    Review N2 & Start N1 Grammar

    Begin by reviewing core N2 grammar, vocabulary (~6,000 words), and 1,000 Kanji to ensure a strong base. Identify weak points from previous mock exams and correct recurring mistakes. Start learning 20–30 N1 grammar patterns, which often include subtle nuances and advanced expressions. Begin reading formal essays, newspaper articles, and academic writing to get accustomed to N1-style texts.

  • Month 2

    Expand Vocabulary & Master Grammar

    Learn 500–600 advanced vocabulary words and 150 Kanji commonly seen at N1 level. Continue studying N1 grammar, focusing on usage and contrast with similar expressions. Strengthen reading skills with texts that include logical arguments, editorial opinions, and policy-related content. Listen to news reports, debates, and lectures to improve comprehension of implicit meaning and abstract topics. Begin working with N1-level reading and listening drills.

  • Month 3

    Deepen Comprehension & Increase Practice

    Add another 500 vocabulary words and 150 Kanji. Complete most of the N1 grammar syllabus and begin weekly review cycles. Practice reading dense academic texts, essays with layered arguments, and documents with formal/journalistic tone. Listen to high-level materials like NHK documentaries, interviews, and conference talks, focusing on tone, implication, and logical flow. Take partial mock exams under time limits to build speed and endurance.

  • Month 4

    Final Review and Full-Length Simulation

    Learn the final 300–400 vocabulary words and 100–150 Kanji. Dedicate this month to reviewing all N1 grammar, refining comprehension of similar and easily confused patterns. Take full-length N1 mock exams weekly under test conditions, analyze your performance in detail, and refine your time management and answering strategies. Continue daily reading and listening with native sources to build fluency, accuracy, and confidence before the exam.
