Plan for N2 level

Focus on this path and you will succeed

  • Month 1

    Reinforcement & Transition to N2

    Revisit all essential N3 content: around 1,000 vocabulary words, 270 Kanji, and core grammar points. Focus on solving N3 mock exams and reviewing mistakes to reinforce your foundation. Begin easing into N2 with 15–20 new grammar patterns and light exposure to intermediate reading and listening materials

  • Month 2

    Building N2 Grammar and Reading Skills

    Learn 350 new vocabulary words, 100 Kanji, and 30–40 grammar points commonly found at N2 level. Focus on reading medium-length texts such as formal emails, articles, and announcements. Practice listening to longer dialogues at near-natural speed, identifying main ideas and context. Begin integrating N2-level practice questions for grammar and vocabulary.

  • Month 3

    Strengthening Comprehension & Test Readiness

    Add 400 vocabulary words and 100 more Kanji. Complete the remaining key N2 grammar points and reinforce them through writing and review. Read opinion essays and short technical passages to improve reading comprehension under time pressure. Train listening with podcasts, interviews, and news clips, focusing on nuance and implied meaning. Start doing mock exam sections for listening and reading weekly.

  • Month 4

    Intensive Review and Exam Simulation

    Finish the last 250 vocabulary words and 100 Kanji. Prioritize reviewing weak points in grammar, vocabulary, and kanji recognition. Take full-length N2 mock exams under timed conditions to improve pacing and accuracy. Analyze your performance to fine-tune test strategies. Continue daily reading and listening practice using native materials to boost fluency and confidence.
